Star Photography

In order to Photograph Stars you will need a high quality carmera that the settings can be adjusted manually. It should be capable of a 30 second shutter speed, and an aperture of about 4.0. You will also need a tripod.

First find a place away from the city where there is little to no light pollution. Then set your camera up on your tripod. Once you have it set up where you want it point it up to the sky. Usually you want something featured in your photo along with the stars, this can be anything from a tree, to people(if you choose people as your subject they must hold really still).The settings you need to set your camera to are as follows:

  • Shutter Speed: 30" (30 seconds)
  • ISO: mid-range, about 800-1600
  • Aperture: In most cases as low as possible (my camera goes to 4.5)
  • If you have auto focus turn it off, and manually focus to infinity, or in other words focus as wide as you can.

As always in photography it will take a little bit of experimenting to get all the settings perfect. You will probably have to focus a little bit less than infinity, but try minimal movements at a time. Practice makes perfect, and if you are lucky maybe you will catch a shooting star!
